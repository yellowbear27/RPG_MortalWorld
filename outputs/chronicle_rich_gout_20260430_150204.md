### Title

A mortal's tale, a thread woven through wealth and tears.

### Chronicle

**Scene One: The Flood**

The young merchant’s family sat on their golden throne of silk, enjoying the luxury that came with wealth in the Song Dynasty. But one day, disaster struck as a sudden flood swept through the market, washing away everything they had worked so hard for. The granaries were emptied, the silk ruined, and even the precious jade coins melted like wax in the torrential rain.

The next morning found them scrambling to rebuild what was left of their shattered lives. They hired new craftsmen to mend their once-grand structures, but the flood left deep scars on their souls as well. The family had been so secure that they had never thought about the future beyond their immediate needs. Now, with everything washed away, they realized it would take more than money and power to keep them in peace.

**Scene Two: The End**

Years passed, and the years seemed to pass without a moment's pause as the merchants struggled to make ends meet. Their children grew up without understanding what had driven their parents' decisions - that everything had been based on wealth alone, and that it was too easy for them to lose control over their lives. But it wasn’t just a financial crisis; it was an identity crisis.

The eldest son, burdened by the expectation of keeping the family name alive through his business acumen, became a rigid man with little emotion or warmth left in him. The youngest daughter, who had inherited her mother’s compassion and desire to help those less fortunate, struggled to find herself in this world where she was expected to become a woman of wealth but not necessarily wisdom.

It was only when the eldest son married into another family for political reasons that he finally started questioning his life’s direction. He realized that money could buy power, but it couldn’t buy happiness or knowledge. His wife and children had always believed in him because of his motherly influence, but now, after years of trying to please her, he felt like a fraud.

On the night before their daughter's wedding, which was to be one of the largest celebrations ever planned by his family, she found herself gout-ridden joints from years of sitting on silk and wine. She had been so focused on maintaining her family’s reputation that she hadn’t noticed how much pain this disease would bring her. The wedding plans were canceled as the merchants realized they couldn’t afford to celebrate without her.

That night, his wife collapsed with unbearable pain in bed, dying just a few hours after the wedding ceremony was planned to start. The son sat by her side until the last breath left her body, and then fell into a deep depression that he would only begin to heal years later through hard-won knowledge about life’s fleeting nature.

He had lost his family business not because of any fault or foolishness, but because he failed to understand how wealth could be used in balance. He realized it was too late for him to change the course of his life, as every decision since childhood had been made out of necessity rather than wisdom. This realization, combined with the inevitability of gout and its debilitating effects on joints over time, sealed the fate that would turn wealth into an outward burden.

### Causal Spine

**Causes:**
1. **Loss of Family Business**: The family’s silk business was wiped out by a flood.
2. **Lack of Adaptation to Future Needs**: With their entire lives centered on maintaining their pre-existing wealth, they failed to adapt as circumstances changed.
3. **Financial Crisis Leading to Identity Crisis**: Their inability to cope with the financial challenges led them into an identity crisis where their focus shifted from making a living to preserving their family’s name and reputation at any cost.

**Consequences:**
1. **Emotional Strain**: Financial difficulties led to emotional strain as they struggled to rebuild their lives.
2. **Self-Destruction of Values**: The lack of adaptation meant that the younger generation lost touch with what was truly important in life, focusing instead on wealth and status.
3. **Physical Pain and Decline**: Health issues like gout became inevitable consequences of living a life focused solely on material success.

The tragedy is not just about personal loss but about the irreversible impact that wealth had on this family’s values, their relationships, and ultimately their very identity as people who valued things other than money and power.
